Reading
Van Houten et al. (1988) — The Right to Effective Behavioral Treatment
JABA, 21(4) · 4 pp · OA

The foundational ethics paper. Eight authors articulate six rights every recipient of behavioral services holds — published in 1988 and still cited every time the field debates effectiveness, restrictiveness, or refusal to treat. Read it once, then read it again with the 2022 Code in front of you.

As you read, hold these in mind
  • Identify the six rights articulated in the paper. Which surprises you?
  • Which 2022 Code citation maps most closely to each right?
  • Where, if at all, does the paper now feel dated 35+ years later?

Assignment 01

Decision-Making Lab 1 — Scope-of-Competence Scenario

5  points

Apply the BACB 11-step decision-making model to the scenario below. Record your response as a Brightspace Discussion Video and post it to the Module 1 discussion thread.

⚜ Scenario

You are a recently certified BCBA working in a community-based ABA agency in Queens. Your supervisor offers you a contract to provide in-home services to a 7-year-old autistic child whose family recently arrived from South Korea. The parents speak limited English and prefer to communicate in Korean. The child has minimal vocal-verbal behavior. You have no formal training in working with culturally and linguistically diverse families, no Korean language proficiency, and limited knowledge of how autism is understood within Korean-American communities. The supervisor tells you, "We don't have anyone else available — and they really need someone."

Your video must address

  1. A restatement of the facts and a list of all relevant stakeholders.
  2. The relevant Code citations — at minimum 1.05 (Scope of Competence), 1.07 (Cultural Responsiveness), 2.01 (Effective Treatment), and 3.04 (Service Agreement). Identify others that apply.
  3. Identification of the relevant Core Principles.
  4. At least three considered courses of action with anticipated consequences for each.
  5. Your selected action and a clear rationale grounded in the Code and Core Principles.
  6. A brief reflection on personal biases or learning history that shaped your analysis (per Code 1.10).

Format

5–7 minute Brightspace Discussion Video, recorded directly in the Brightspace discussion thread (no separate upload). Cite Code sections by number as you discuss them — verbal citations are fine. Visual aids (e.g., a slide briefly showing key Code citations) are welcome but not required.

Assignment 02

Toolkit Artifact 1 — Scope-of-Competence Map

2  points

Build the first artifact of your Practitioner Ethics Toolkit — a self-assessment map you'll carry into fieldwork and supervision.

The map must include

  • Current scope. Areas of competence you can defensibly claim now (training + supervised hours + outcomes).
  • Developing scope. Areas where you have some training but are not yet independently competent — identify what supervision or training would close the gap.
  • Out of scope. Areas that are clearly beyond your competence and how you would handle a referral or refusal.
  • Cultural & population considerations. CLD groups, age ranges, disability profiles, and settings where your scope has specific limitations or strengths.
  • Action plan. One concrete step you'll take in the next 90 days to expand your scope ethically.

Format

1 page, any layout. Will be graded on a Met / Approaching / Not Yet rubric. A template is provided in Brightspace if you'd like a starting structure — feel free to design your own.
