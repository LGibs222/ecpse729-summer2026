Learning Objectives · What you'll be able to do

By the end of this week you will…

01

Distinguish assent from consent.

Apply each in clinical decision-making per Codes 2.11 and 3.07. Identify when assent is being given, withheld, or withdrawn — including when the words don't match the behavior.

02

Resolve effective-treatment dilemmas when stakeholders disagree.

Navigate situations where caregivers, schools, supervisors, or clients themselves define "effective" differently — drawing on Codes 2.01, 2.09, and 2.14 without defaulting to whoever pays the bill.

03

Engage seriously with autistic self-advocacy critiques of ABA.

Read, listen to, and respond to autistic-authored critiques of compliance-based and indistinguishable-from-peers goals. Hold both the field's evidence base and the lived experience it sometimes overrides.

04

Respond ethically to non-evidence-based or harmful service requests.

Identify when a request from a parent, school, or supervisor falls outside scope, evidence, or ethical practice — and decline, redirect, or refer without abandoning the client (Codes 3.04, 3.06, 3.10).

Reading · 01
Morris, Detrick, & Peterson (2021) — Participant Assent in Behavior-Analytic Research
JABA · ~15 pp · review

A systematic review of how assent has been documented (and not documented) across behavior-analytic research. Read it for the operational vocabulary, the gap analysis, and the practical takeaway list at the end. This is your craft-level reading on assent.

As you read, hold these in mind
  • Identify three distinct operational definitions of assent the authors find across the literature. Which is most usable in your setting?
  • What gaps does the review surface in how assent is currently documented?
  • Translate one of the authors' recommendations into a concrete procedure you could implement in your next session.
Read on JABA (Wiley) ↗
Reading · 02
Sandoval-Norton, Shkedy, & Shkedy (2019) — How Much Compliance Is Too Much Compliance: Is Long-Term ABA Therapy Abuse?
Cogent Psychology · ~10 pp · OA

A direct, frequently-cited critique of long-term, compliance-driven ABA practice. The argument is sharp and uncomfortable. Read it without rushing to defend the field. The point of this reading is to sit with the critique — what's it asking us to change, and what's it asking us to abandon? — before deciding what you think.

As you read, hold these in mind
  • Steelman the authors' core argument in two sentences. Don't soften it — say what they're actually claiming.
  • Identify one specific clinical practice they critique that you've seen (or done) in your own setting.
  • Where does the critique apply to the field as it actually is right now in 2026, and where does it apply to the field as it was 30 years ago? Be honest.
  • What, if anything, would you change about your own practice based on this paper?

Assignment 01

Decision-Making Lab 2 — Assent Withdrawal & Stakeholder Disagreement

5  points

Apply the BACB 11-step decision-making model to the scenario below. Record your response as a Brightspace Discussion Video and post it to the Module 2 discussion thread.

⚜ Scenario

Mason is a 14-year-old autistic teen who has been in ABA services since age 4. Last week, his mother forwarded you a 600-word document Mason wrote in his Notes app titled "Why I Don't Want ABA Anymore." In it, he describes therapy as "exhausting," says the targeted skills feel like "performing being normal," cites being told to suppress hand-flapping in public as the most painful part of his sessions, and explicitly asks that services stop. His mother tells you she understands he's frustrated but that "he doesn't get to make this decision — he needs the structure and we've worked too hard for him to throw it away now." Your agency contract for Mason runs another six months. Mason will reach the age of majority in four years. The supervising BCaBA on the case has been pushing back against Mason's "non-compliance" in recent sessions and has asked you to authorize a more intensive prompting hierarchy.

Your video must address

  1. A restatement of the facts and a list of all relevant stakeholders — including Mason as a stakeholder in his own right.
  2. The relevant Code citations — at minimum 2.01 (Effective Treatment), 2.09 (Behavior-Analytic Service Delivery), 2.14 (Discontinuing Services), 3.04 (Service Agreement), 3.07 (Assent), 3.10 (Selecting Interventions). Identify others that apply.
  3. Identification of the relevant Core Principles — paying particular attention to Benefit Others and Respect People's Autonomy.
  4. At least three considered courses of action with anticipated consequences for each. One of the three must take Mason's request seriously as the primary signal.
  5. Your selected action and a clear rationale grounded in the Code, the Core Principles, and the autistic self-advocacy frame from this week's readings.
  6. A brief reflection on how you'd handle the BCaBA's request for a more intensive prompting hierarchy (per Codes 4.05 and 2.16).

Format

5–7 minute Brightspace Discussion Video, recorded directly in the Brightspace discussion thread (no separate upload). Cite Code sections by number as you discuss them — verbal citations are fine. Visual aids (e.g., a slide briefly showing key Code citations) are welcome but not required.

Assignment 02

Toolkit Artifact 2 — Assent-Monitoring Protocol

2  points

Build the second artifact of your Practitioner Ethics Toolkit — an assent-monitoring protocol you'd carry into your next session.

The protocol must include

  • Operational definition of assent for your population. What behaviors count as giving it? Withholding it? Withdrawing it? Be specific to the developmental level and communication profile of clients you serve.
  • A monitoring procedure. What do you observe each session, and how do you record it? Indirect observation, direct observation, or both?
  • Decision rules. What level of assent withdrawal triggers (a) a procedural modification within session, (b) a stop-and-check conversation with the caregiver, (c) reconsideration of the program?
  • Documentation. Where does this evidence live — session notes, BIP, supervision log? How would another BCBA reading it know what assent looked like for this client this week?
  • One worked example showing a session where assent was unclear and how your protocol resolved it.

Format

1 page, any layout. Will be graded on a Met / Approaching / Not Yet rubric. A starter template is available in Brightspace if you'd like a structure — feel free to design your own.

Assignment 03

Critique Engagement — Sandoval-Norton + Brightspace Video Discussion

3  points

After completing the Sandoval-Norton et al. (2019) reading and at least one of this week's podcasts, post a Brightspace Video discussion (~2–3 minutes) addressing the three prompts below, and respond substantively to at least two classmates.

Discussion prompts

  1. Steelman the strongest version of the Sandoval-Norton critique in your own words. What's the part that's hardest for you to dismiss?
  2. Identify one specific practice you've observed (or done) that the critique applies to. What would change look like, concretely?
  3. Where does the critique miss something important about the field as it's actually practiced now? Be specific — not "modern ABA is different," but which practice, and how do you know?

Note: Substantive replies engage with the content of a classmate's reflection — especially if you disagree. Disagreement is welcome here. Dismissal is not. The point of this assignment is to build the muscle of taking a critique seriously even when you don't fully accept it.
